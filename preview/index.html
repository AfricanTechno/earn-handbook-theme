    Operational Resilience Review

    Defines the monthly review pattern used by entity leaders to verify the health of critical services, decide on escalation, and maintain an evidence trail strong enough for group-level oversight.

    Authority owner
    Chief Operating Officer
    Provider
    Operational Excellence Office
    Applicable entities
    earnOS, atOS, agOS, aghOS, alpOS, ebsOS

    The monthly resilience review is not a status meeting. It is the decision point where service owners, control owners, and executives confirm whether each critical service remains within acceptable operating limits and whether the recorded evidence is sufficient for internal or external scrutiny.

    Why this matters

    Each entity runs different systems, but the review pattern stays consistent. A shared structure reduces ambiguity, improves comparability across entities, and gives escalation paths a clear trigger instead of relying on instinct.

    • Surface weakening indicators before they become customer-visible incidents.
    • Keep accountability attached to named authority and provider owners.
    • Ensure the group handbook can compare evidence on the same basis across entities.

    Decision model

    Reviewers classify every critical service using a simple three-state model: healthy, watch, or escalate. The model works only if supporting evidence is no older than the current review cycle.

    A review is incomplete when owners can describe a risk but cannot point to the latest proof.

    Weekly operational cadence

    Teams collect weekly operational signals so the monthly review can stay decision-focused. The monthly pack should summarize trend changes rather than replay every event in chronological order. 

    Inputs required before the review
- service health snapshot
- major incident summary
- open control remediation items
- evidence links for changed controls

    Escalation signals

    Escalation is required when service degradation persists beyond the entity's tolerance window, when a control has no valid owner, or when a material gap cannot be evidenced within the review cycle.

    Signal Threshold Required action
    Critical service health Two consecutive weeks below target Escalate to entity leadership and open a remediation plan
    Control evidence age Older than the current review cycle Move the service to watch until evidence is refreshed
    Owner coverage Authority or provider owner missing Block closure and assign named ownership

    Quarterly evidence pack

    The monthly review should reference live evidence, while the quarterly pack preserves the exact basis for the decisions taken during the period. This distinction keeps the working review lightweight without losing auditability.

    Control coverage and mapping

    Every service reviewed must map to the minimum control set defined by the group. Missing mappings are tracked openly and carried until the next review with a named remediation owner.

    1. Confirm the service inventory is complete.
    2. Verify the mapped control set is still current.
    3. Check that each control has recent evidence and a named owner.
    4. Record deviations with remediation dates.

    Authority and provider owners

    Authority owners decide acceptability. Provider owners decide delivery changes. The review fails when one person tries to substitute for both roles without explicit delegation.
